# Death Scenario

## The Death (Summary)
**Method:** Aconitine poisoning from wild monkshood (torikabuto) mistaken for edible sansai (mountain vegetables)
**Category:** Circumstantial/Random
**Duration:** Hours (4-6 hours from ingestion to death)
**Witnesses:** None initially, found by hostel staff

## Mechanism (Technical)

### The Cause
Aconitine toxicity from Aconitum japonicum (Japanese monkshood/torikabuto). Affects sodium channels in nerves and heart. Causes progressive paralysis, arrhythmia, and cardiac arrest. No antidote.

### The Setup
Rural mountain area has patches of wild monkshood growing near edible plants. A warning sign uses complex kanji. Madi recognizes some characters and guesses (wrongly) at the meaning. The sign actually says "DANGER - POISONOUS PLANT - DO NOT TOUCH OR EAT."

### The Trigger
Madi harvests monkshood leaves and roots, believing them to be shidoke (edible mountain vegetable). Returns to hostel, prepares them as tempura following a recipe she half-understands. Eats her "authentic" meal.

### The Process
1. Minute 0-30: Tingling in mouth and tongue - she thinks it's from hot oil
2. Hour 1: Numbness spreading, nausea beginning
3. Hour 2: Vomiting, dizziness, realizes something is very wrong
4. Hour 3: Paralysis beginning, breathing difficult, manages to stumble toward hostel
5. Hour 4-6: Cardiac arrhythmia, collapse, found by staff
6. Hour 6: Dies in ambulance or shortly after reaching hospital

## Research Notes
- Monkshood poisoning occurs regularly in Japan - mistaken foraging
- As few as 2-4 mg of aconitine can be fatal
- Death typically occurs 1-6 hours after ingestion
- Symptoms: tingling, numbness, nausea, vomiting, paralysis, arrhythmia
- Source: Japanese poison control case studies
- Source: Botanical toxicology literature

## The Death Sequence (Full Detail)

### Setup (Chapter 35-37)
Madi has been at the hostel for two days, loving the "authentic" experience. She's shown competence (in her mind) by communicating in Japanese. The foraging guide is busy with another group. She decides to explore on her own.

### Trigger (Chapter 38)
She finds the patch with the warning sign. She studies the sign. She recognizes: "mountain" and "plant." She interprets two unfamiliar kanji using context clues (wrongly). She harvests. She photographs her haul proudly.

### The Death (Chapters 39-40)
Write this sequence in full detail:

**Hour 0-1:**
Back at hostel, Madi prepares her tempura. She's filmed some of the process for content. First bite: odd tingle. Second bite: definitely tingly. She dismisses it - "wild food is intense." Within 30 minutes, her lips are numb. She stops eating but the damage is done.

**Hour 2-3:**
Nausea hits hard. She vomits everything. The numbness spreads down her arms, up her face. She tries to use her phone but her fingers won't cooperate. She stumbles toward the common room. Her heart is doing something wrong - she can feel it stuttering.

**Hour 4-5:**
She collapses in the hallway. A staff member finds her, pale and barely conscious. She tries to explain - "shokubutsu... tabeta..." (plant... ate...). They call an ambulance. In the mountains, it takes time. Her heartbeat becomes erratic. Her breathing is shallow.

**Final Moment:**
In the ambulance or upon arrival at the small rural hospital, Madison Chen's heart gives out. The doctors know immediately it's aconitine - they've seen it before. The local foraging accidents. The tourists who don't read the signs. She's gone before they can try any treatment.

### Immediate Aftermath
Her phone, still unlocked, shows the photos of her harvest. The wrong plants, bright and deadly. Her last story is her preparing them, caption: "Farm to table doesn't get more real than this!" The hostel is investigated. The sign was adequate - she simply couldn't read it.

## Sensory Details

### Sight
The beautiful mountain plants. The warning sign she misread. Her own hands, increasingly unable to move. The fluorescent lights of the ambulance.

### Sound
The sizzle of tempura. Her own heartbeat, increasingly wrong. Japanese voices she can finally understand - "dame" (bad), "abunai" (dangerous) - words she knew all along.

### Touch/Pain
The progressive numbness, which is terrifying in its absence of sensation. The loss of control over her body. The ice-cold feeling spreading through her limbs.

### Smell
Fresh mountain air. Cooking oil. Her own vomit.

### Taste
The bitter, tingling flavor she mistook for "wild" and "authentic." The taste that should have warned her.

## The Horror Element
The horror is recognition: Madi finally realizes what the sign said in her last hours. The kanji becomes clear in her poisoned mind. She knew the words. She just didn't know them well enough. She killed herself with partial knowledge, which is more dangerous than no knowledge at all.

## The Comedy Element
She documented the entire thing. Her TikTok shows her harvesting, cooking, and eating the poison. Her caption brags about her language skills. Her community will watch their "basically fluent" friend eat her way to death while smiling at the camera.

## Writing Notes

### Pacing the Death
Medium build. The foraging sequence is peaceful, almost idyllic. The eating sequence is cozy. Then the horror kicks in and accelerates. The last hours are frantic, panicked, tragic.

### POV During Death
Stay with Madi throughout. The dawning horror of realizing her mistake. The irony of finally understanding Japanese when it's too late.

### Graphic Content Level
- 6/10 - Poisoning symptoms are described but focused on the experience rather than gore
- Show: the psychological journey from confidence to terror. Imply: the physical degradation.
